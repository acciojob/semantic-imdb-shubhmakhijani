Summary

In 1947, Andy Dufresne (Tim Robbins), a banker in Maine, is convicted of murdering his wife and her lover, a crime he didn't commit. He's sentenced to two consecutive life sentences at Shawshank State Penitentiary. Andy befriends Ellis "Red" Redding (Morgan Freeman), and over the years, Andy uses his banking skills to gain favor with the prison staff, particularly the corrupt warden. Despite the brutal reality of prison life, Andy maintains hope and eventually executes an elaborate escape plan, exposing the warden's corruption before fleeing to Mexico.

Actors

Tim Robbins as Andy Dufresne - A banker wrongfully convicted of murder who maintains his innocence and dignity throughout his imprisonment.

Morgan Freeman as Ellis Boyd "Red" Redding - The narrator of the story and Andy's closest friend in prison.

Bob Gunton as Warden Norton - The corrupt warden of Shawshank who exploits prisoners for personal gain.

William Sadler as Heywood - One of Red's friends and a member of their prison group.

Clancy Brown as Captain Hadley - The brutal head guard of Shawshank.
